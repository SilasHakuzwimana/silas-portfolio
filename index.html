I am a Year III Computer & Software Engineering student at the University of Rwanda - College of Science and Technology (CST), and I build things that matter. My career path took an uncommon route. Before writing a line of production code, I stood in front of students as an acting Physics and Mathematics teacher at GS Kagasa/Ngeruka and acting Entrepreneurship and General Studies and Communication Studies (GS&CS) teacher at GS Mareba. When I identified that the school's administrative inefficiencies were costing student outcomes, I didn't wait — I built the GS Mareba IMS, a full digital management system that helped push the school's success rate to 97% and 95% respectively.

As Class Representative at UR-CST, I translate that same instinct — seeing systemic gaps and closing them — into student governance and peer advocacy. I am not just an engineer; I am an architect of better systems, whether those systems are networks, software, or organizations.

Online Transport System — Automating Rwanda's Logistics Layer

Logistics management in Rwanda faced a structural bottleneck: manual vehicle scheduling created fragmented, error-prone data across fleet operations. The OTS was commissioned to replace this entirely with a secure, real-time digital platform.

I architected the system around three core pillars: a scalable Laravel backend with custom middleware for secure data handling, a reactive Vue.js + Tailwind CSS dashboard for real-time fleet visualization without page reloads, and DSA-optimized routing algorithms that ensure the system remains fast as operational data grows exponentially.

"By integrating modern web frameworks with systematic network security, OTS serves as a benchmark for how local logistics can be transformed through professional software engineering."
